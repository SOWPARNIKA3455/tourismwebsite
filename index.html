About Us

Kerala, located on India's southwestern coast, is known as "God's Own Country" for its stunning natural beauty. It features serene backwaters, lush hill stations, sandy beaches, and dense forests. The state is rich in cultural heritage, offering traditional dance forms like Kathakali and festivals like Onam. Kerala is the birthplace of Ayurveda, offering rejuvenating treatments. Popular destinations include Munnar, Kochi, Alleppey, and Wayanad. The cuisine is known for its use of coconut, seafood, and spices. Kerala is famous for its houseboat cruises and wildlife sanctuaries. It has a tropical climate, best visited from October to February. Kerala's diverse landscape caters to nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those looking for relaxation. It is a hub for eco-tourism and cultural exploration.

Top Destinations in Kerala

Alleppey

Known for its backwaters and houseboat cruises, Alleppey offers a serene and peaceful escape.

Munnar

Famous for its tea plantations and mist-covered hills, Munnar is perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Kochi

A city blending colonial history with modern attractions, Kochi offers diverse cultural experiences.

Kovalam

Known for its beautiful beaches, Kovalam is the ideal spot for relaxation and water sports.

Athirapally Waterfall

It is the largest waterfall in Kerala, which stands tall at 81.5 feet. Just a short drive from Athirapilly is the Vazhachal Falls.

Jadayu-Earth center

The spot is famous for the giant rock sculpture of Jatayu, the mythical bird of the epic Ramayana.

sree padmanabhaswamy temple

Sree Padmanabha Swamy Temple in Thiruvananthapuram district is one of the oldest and famous shrines in Kerala.

Thekkady-periyar

Periyar Tiger Reserve located in Thekkady in the district of Idukki, is one of the 27 tiger reserves in India.

Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort, located in the Kasaragod district of Kerala, is one of the largest and best-preserved forts in the state.

Districts of Kerala

  • Thiruvananthapuram, also known as Trivandrum, is the capital city of the Indian state of Kerala. It is located on the southwestern coast of India, along the Arabian Sea. The city is known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and beautiful natural surroundings.

  • Kollam is a city located in the state of Kerala, India, situated along the Malabar Coast. It is one of the oldest ports in the country and holds significant historical and cultural importance.

  • Pathanamthitta is a city and district located in the central part of Kerala, India. It is known for its natural beauty, historical significance, and cultural heritage.

  • Kottayam is a scenic district located in the central part of Kerala, India. It is known for its picturesque landscapes, rich cultural heritage, historical significance, and thriving agricultural sector. The district is a prominent hub of Kerala’s rubber industry and plays a key role in the state’s agriculture and literature.

  • Idukki is a picturesque district located in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India. Known for its stunning landscapes, wildlife, and high-altitude regions, Idukki is a popular destination for nature lovers, trekkers, and those looking for a peaceful retreat. The district is famous for its lush green hills, tea plantations, waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries.

  • Ernakulam, often considered the commercial capital of Kerala, is a major district and city located on the southwestern coast of India. It is known for its vibrant cultural scene, rich history, and economic significance. Ernakulam serves as the heart of the bustling city of Kochi, one of Kerala’s most important urban centers.

  • Thrissur, often called the cultural capital of Kerala, is a city and district in central Kerala, India. Known for its rich history, vibrant festivals, religious significance, and cultural heritage, Thrissur holds a unique place in the hearts of Keralites.

  • Palakkad is a picturesque district located in the central part of Kerala, India. Often referred to as the "Gateway of Kerala," it is a major entry point into the state, due to its location near the Western Ghats and its proximity to Tamil Nadu. Palakkad is famous for its rich history, lush landscapes, and cultural diversity.

  • Malappuram is a vibrant and culturally rich district in the southern state of Kerala, India. Located in the northern part of the state, Malappuram is known for its diverse population, beautiful landscapes, historical significance, and religious importance.

  • Kozhikode (formerly known as Calicut) is a major city in the northern part of Kerala, India. It is the headquarters of the Kozhikode district and one of the most significant urban centers in the state. Kozhikode has a rich historical, cultural, and commercial significance, and it is well known for its natural beauty, vibrant cuisine, and historical landmarks.

  • Wayanad is a picturesque district in the northeastern part of Kerala, India. Known for its natural beauty, rich biodiversity, and serene atmosphere, it is a popular tourist destination. The district is part of the Western Ghats and is known for its cool climate, lush forests, tea plantations, and historical sites.

  • Kannur is a city in the northern part of Kerala, India. It serves as the administrative headquarters of the Kannur district. Known for its rich cultural heritage, Kannur is famous for its historical significance, natural beauty, and vibrant traditions.

  • Kasaragod is a district and city located in the northernmost part of Kerala, India. Known for its cultural diversity, natural beauty, and historical significance, it offers a unique blend of coastal and hill landscapes.
